Propofol

IV Anesthetic
Aromatic Ring Phenol —OH Isopropyl ×2

Ethanol
C₂H₅OH · Simplest Alcohol
Reference · Alcohol
Alcohol —OH Short chain C2
Read it: The —OH makes it miscible with water. Short carbon chain = volatile. The reference alcohol for understanding the group.

Local Anesthetic
Aromatic Ring Amide C=O Tertiary Amine
Read it: Amide bond = not cleaved by plasma enzymes = long-acting. Amine = membrane crossing mechanism. Ring = tissue penetration.
Sevoflurane
Ultane · Fluoromethyl ether
Inhaled Anesthetic
Fluorine ×7 Ether —O— Tiny Skeleton C4
Read it: 7 fluorines = almost no blood solubility = drug rushes to brain and rushes back out. Fastest, cleanest emergence of any common anesthetic.
Fentanyl
Sublimaze · 100× Morphine
Opioid Analgesic
Aromatic Ring ×2 Tertiary Amine Carbonyl C=O
Read it: Two phenyl rings = extremely fat-soluble = penetrates brain in minutes. Amine = opioid receptor binding. No —OH = more lipophilic than morphine.
Succinylcholine
Anectine · SCh · RSI Drug
NMB · Depolarizing
Ester —COO— ×2 Quaternary Amine N⁺ ×2
Read it: Two ester bonds = two countdown timers. Permanent positive charge = never leaves the NMJ. The molecule is its own off switch.
Morphine
MS Contin · Reference Opioid · C₁₇H₁₉NO₃
Opioid · Reference Standard
Aromatic Ring Phenolic —OH Alcoholic —OH Ether —O— N-Methyl Amine 5 Fused Rings
Read it: Two —OH groups = more water-soluble than fentanyl = slower brain entry = slower onset. N-methyl amine = opioid receptor binding. Complex fused ring system = long duration (4–6 hrs). M6G metabolite is more potent — accumulates in renal failure.
