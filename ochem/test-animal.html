1
Propofol
Sleepy Axolotl 🦎
IV Induction · GABA-A
Visual Mnemonic
Why the axolotl
Isopropyl "ears/gills" = bulky arms slow metabolism
Phenol "mouth" —OH = makes it injectable
Benzene "body" = rapid blood-brain barrier crossing
Gentle, dreamy — Milk of Amnesia
Real Structure · 2,6-Diisopropylphenol
Clinical Pearls
Rapid onset & emergence — typical induction dose 1.5–2.5 mg/kg
Watch for hypotension on induction — vasodilatory effect
Propofol infusion syndrome with prolonged high-dose infusions
White lipid emulsion — don't leave open, contamination risk
2
Ethanol
Goofy Balloon Dog 🐩
Reference Alcohol · C₂H₅OH
Visual Mnemonic
Why the balloon dog
C–C zigzag = the bendy balloon neck
—OH = the goofy nose, makes it an alcohol
Simple, lightweight, goes everywhere water goes
The reference creature for all alcohols
Real Structure · CH₃—CH₂—OH
Clinical Pearls
Reference molecule for the alcohol functional group
CNS depressant via GABA enhancement — same target as many anesthetics
—OH group = miscible with water, dissolves in blood readily
Short chain = volatile at room temperature
3
Lidocaine
Arrow Missing the Target 🎯
Local Anesthetic · Amide
Visual Mnemonic
Why the focused arrow
Ring = arrowhead piercing into nerve tissue
Amide shaft = long-lasting (resists plasma esterases)
Amine tip = changes charge mid-flight to land the block
Has to find exactly the right pH to hit the sodium channel
Real Structure · Ring–Amide–Amine
Clinical Pearls
Amide = NOT cleaved by plasma esterases → 1–2 hr duration
Also antiarrhythmic via the same Na⁺ channel mechanism
pKa 7.9 — works better in normal tissue pH; infected tissue (acidic) reduces efficacy
Max dose ~4.5 mg/kg plain, 7 mg/kg with epinephrine
4
Sevoflurane
Broken-Rake Gremlin 🪣
Volatile Inhaled Agent
Visual Mnemonic
Why the broken rake
7 fluorine "tines" = wild spiky hair in every direction
Sharp, volatile — barely dissolves in blood
In fast, out fast — no mess left behind
The gremlin that tidies up as it leaves
Real Structure · F₃C–CHF–O–CH₂F
Clinical Pearls
Blood:gas coefficient 0.65 — fastest common inhalation agent
Ideal for pediatric mask inductions — non-pungent, sweet smell
Compound A production in low-flow circuits — use ≥2 L/min fresh gas
Cleanest, fastest emergence of modern volatile agents
5
Fentanyl
Stern Traffic-Light Guardian 🚦
Opioid · 100× Morphine
Visual Mnemonic
Why the traffic light
Two stacked phenyl rings = the warning lights
N-carbonyl post = anchors it to the receptor
A STOP signal for pain — and for breathing
The sternest molecule in your cart
Real Structure · 2 Phenyl Rings + Piperidine N
Clinical Pearls
100× more potent than morphine — dose in micrograms
Chest wall rigidity ("wooden chest") at high doses — treat with NMB
Context-sensitive half-time rises with prolonged infusion — accumulates in fat
Reverse with naloxone — but fentanyl outlasts short-acting naloxone
6
Succinylcholine
Strained Pull-Up Bar Creature 🏋️
Depolarizing NMB · RSI
Visual Mnemonic
Why the pull-up bar
Two N⁺ "hands" gripping each end symmetrically
Ester bonds = grip strength — plasma enzymes peel fingers back
When both esters are cleaved, you drop — no choice
Cannot hold on longer than the molecule allows
Real Structure · Two Choline Units · Two Esters
Clinical Pearls
Fastest NMB onset — intubating conditions in ~60 sec
Cannot be reversed — wait for plasma pseudocholinesterases
Hyperkalemia risk — avoid in burns, crush injuries, prolonged immobility
Malignant hyperthermia trigger — know your dantrolene protocol
7
Morphine
Cozy Sleeping Caterpillar 🐛
Opioid · Reference Standard
Visual Mnemonic
Why the sleeping caterpillar
5 fused ring "segments" = the caterpillar body
Phenolic OH = soft pillow the head rests on
Doesn't rush — slow onset, long heavy sleep
The original, the reference, the classic
Real Structure · 5 Fused Rings · C₁₇H₁₉NO₃
Clinical Pearls
More water-soluble than fentanyl → slower BBB crossing → slower onset
M6G metabolite is MORE potent — dangerous accumulation in renal failure
Histamine release with rapid IV push — give slowly
The reference opioid — all others compared to morphine equivalents
