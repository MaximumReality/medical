◈ CLINICAL LAB CODEX

STATION TERMINAL: MEDICAL OVERRIDE ACTIVE
WBC (White Blood Cells) 4.5 - 11.0 K/µL
High levels (Leukocytosis) indicate infection or inflammation. Low levels (Leukopenia) suggest immunocompromise.
Nursing Action: Monitor for fever/chills. Check "Differential" for Neutrophil count. Initiate Neutropenic Precautions if low.
Hemoglobin (Hgb) 12 - 18 g/dL
Determines blood's oxygen-carrying capacity. Critical low indicates severe anemia or active hemorrhage.
Nursing Action: If < 7.0 g/dL, prepare for blood transfusion protocol. Monitor O2 saturation and heart rate.
Platelets (PLT) 150 - 450 K/µL
Essential for blood clotting. Low levels (Thrombocytopenia) lead to spontaneous bruising and bleeding.
Nursing Action: **BLEEDING PRECAUTIONS.** Use electric razors, soft toothbrushes. Monitor for petechiae (red spots).
Potassium (K+) 3.5 - 5.0 mEq/L
The "Cardiac Electrolyte." Essential for nerve conduction and muscle contraction in the heart.
Nursing Action: CRITICAL RISK. Both High/Low cause arrhythmias. GET EKG IMMEDIATELY. Never IV Push.
Creatinine 0.6 - 1.2 mg/dL
The gold standard for Kidney function. Elevation indicates renal failure or severe dehydration.
Nursing Action: Monitor Intake/Output. If high, notify MD before giving nephrotoxic meds (like NSAIDs or IV contrast).
